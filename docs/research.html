  • Highest-conviction value/growth is META; highest quality is MSFT; highest risk is TSLA, MU and INTC. Risk scores reflect the range of outcomes, not company prestige.

Portfolio-Construction Framework

You are open to anything, so rather than a single buy list, here is a way to size these nine by risk tier. The key principle: position size should scale inversely with the risk score — do not equal-weight a 3/10 compounder and a 9/10 turnaround.

Tier 1 — Core compounders (own the business): MSFT, GOOGL, AMZN, AAPL

Durable moats, double-digit (or buyback-driven) growth, strong balance sheets, reasonable-to-full valuations. The ballast of the book — appropriate for dollar-cost-averaging and larger weights.

Tier 2 — Growth at higher volatility: NVDA, META

The best growth (NVDA) and the best value-growth trade-off (META) on the screen — but more cyclical and headline-sensitive. Meaningful but volatility-sized weights; prefer pullback entries.

Tier 3 — High-risk / tactical (cycle & turnaround): TSLA, MU, INTC

Outsized outcome ranges — extreme valuation (TSLA), peak-cycle commodity (MU), binary turnaround (INTC). If owned at all: small, clearly-sized positions with hard stops and valuation/cycle-aware entries.

Across all three tiers the disciplines are the same: enter in zones (not at any price), define the stop before you buy, and size each position so the stop-loss is a survivable loss for the portfolio.
