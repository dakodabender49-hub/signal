How to Read These Scans

  • Relative volume (RVOL). Today's volume divided by the 20-day average. Above ~1.5x means unusual participation — moves on high RVOL tend to be more meaningful than moves on quiet volume.
  • Breakout. Price closed at a 20-day high on elevated volume. The trigger is acceptance above the breakout level; the invalidation is a close back below it.
  • RS leader. Outperforming the S&P 500 over ~3 months and trading near its own highs — relative strength, the tendency of strong names to stay strong.
  • Golden pocket. Price is sitting in the 0.618–0.65 Fibonacci retracement of the active swing — a common area for trend continuation to resume or fail.
  • Trend reclaim. Price has crossed back above a key moving average (e.g., the 200-day), a structural shift worth watching.
  • Trigger / Invalidation. The engine's objective "line in the sand" each way — the level that confirms the setup and the level that voids it. Define risk against the invalidation before acting.
