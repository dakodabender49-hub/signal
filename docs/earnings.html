The Setup

Micron enters its fiscal Q3 print as the purest large-cap proxy for the AI-memory shortage. High-bandwidth memory (HBM) is reportedly sold out through 2026 on multi-year hyperscaler agreements, DRAM and NAND pricing is climbing, and gross margin has roughly doubled in four quarters (39% → ~81% guided). The stock has compounded violently — up roughly 750% in a year to ~$1,052. That sets a high bar: management has guided Q3 to record $33.5B revenue, ~81% gross margin, and $19.15 EPS, and the Street sits just above. The debate is no longer "is it good" — it is "how much of the good news is already in the price," and whether the FQ4 guide can extend a parabolic run.

What Wall Street Is Watching

  • HBM revenue & HBM4 ramp. HBM3E is reportedly sold out through 2026 on multi-year deals; the swing question is HBM4 timing, yields and pricing, plus share vs. SK Hynix and Samsung.
  • DRAM & NAND pricing (ASPs). The entire thesis is pricing. Any commentary that bit-pricing is decelerating — even at a high level — matters more than the headline beat.
  • Data-center mix. Cloud Memory + Core Data Center were ~$13.4B (56% of FQ2 revenue). The market wants the AI-levered mix to keep climbing.
  • Gross margin ceiling. Guided ~81% from 39% a year ago. How much higher mix and pricing can push margin is the key upside lever — and the thing most exposed if pricing rolls.
  • FQ4'26 guide. The real catalyst. The print is largely known; the forward guide decides the reaction. Buy-side whispers are well above the formal Street number.
  • Supply discipline & capex. The three-player oligopoly's restraint is what sustains pricing. Heavy capex that signals a supply flood would be a bearish tell.
  • Inventory (DIO). Lean channel inventory keeps pricing power intact; any build is an early-cycle warning.

Demand posture. HBM sold out through 2026; investing in global manufacturing footprint to meet "growing demand." No indication of supply loosening — the message was scarcity, not normalization.

Bull Case vs. Bear Case

Bull — the ramp extends +12 to +15%

Micron beats the guide again (as it has every quarter for two years), revenue clears ~$34–35B and gross margin pushes past 81%. The decisive piece: an FQ4 guide raised sharply, confirming HBM4 momentum and sold-out supply through 2026. If it clears the implied move to the upside, ~$1,180–$1,210 on the session, and the ~9x forward multiple looks far too cheap for the growth. Longer-run scenario target on the research page: $1,400.

Bear — "as good as it gets" −12 to −20%

The quarter beats but the stock fades — the documented pattern (down in 6 of 8). An FQ4 guide that is merely in-line with the formal Street (i.e., below the lofty buy-side whisper), any hint of decelerating bit-pricing, an inventory build, or a capex signal that supply is coming — any one re-rates a +750% name fast. Through the implied move that is ~$840–$925; the research-page cycle-bear scenario is $650 if the cycle rolls.

Memory is the most cyclical corner of semiconductors. The asymmetry here is not about this quarter's number — it is about whether the forward guide can keep beating an already-euphoric bar. Define risk before the print; the implied move is wide for a reason.
