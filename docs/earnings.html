The Print — What Happened

Micron didn't just beat — it cleared its own guide by ~24% on revenue and ~31% on EPS, posted a record 84.9% gross margin, and then raised the bar dramatically with an FQ4 guide of $50.0B revenue and $31.00 EPS. Management also disclosed 16 Strategic Customer Agreements representing ~$100B of minimum contracted revenue — reframing a notoriously cyclical business as something with multi-year visibility. The stock, which had drifted into the print, jumped 13–15% after hours.

Going In — The Setup (before)

Micron entered the print as the purest large-cap proxy for the AI-memory shortage: HBM reportedly sold out through 2026, DRAM/NAND pricing climbing, and gross margin already up from ~39% to a guided ~81% in a year. The stock had compounded ~750% to ~$1,052. The Street sat at ~$19.9 EPS / ~$34.5B revenue versus the company's own guide of $19.15 / $33.5B / ~81% GM. Options priced a large ~±12% move, and the open question was whether a known-good quarter could clear an already-euphoric bar — particularly the forward guide. It did, decisively.

What Drove It

  • The $100B disclosure. 16 multi-year Strategic Customer Agreements representing ~$100B of minimum contracted revenue — the headline. It reframes Micron from a price-taking commodity cyclical toward contracted, visible demand, which is why the multiple held up into a +750% run.
  • HBM4 is shipping. HBM4 (on 1-beta DRAM) is in high-volume shipments for the lead customer's platform, with qualification samples out to multiple end-customers; HBM4E (1-gamma) targets volume production in calendar 2027. The roadmap is executing, not slipping.
  • Margin melt-up. Non-GAAP gross margin hit a record 84.9% and is guided to ~86% — extraordinary for memory, driven by pricing, mix and cost-down. Core Data Center and Mobile & Client posted 87% gross margins.
  • Data center is now the company. Cloud Memory + Core Data Center = ~$25.3B (~61% of revenue), up from ~$4.9B a year ago.
  • Fortress balance sheet. $30.2B cash & investments vs ~$5.7B debt (net cash ~$24B); $18.3B adjusted free cash flow in the quarter funds a record capex ramp without strain.

FQ4'26 Guidance & Management

The guide that moved the stock. Revenue $50.0B ± $1.0B, gross margin ~86%, non-GAAP EPS $31.00 ± $1.00, opex ~$1.65B — a sequential step-up of ~+21% revenue and ~+23% EPS on top of a record quarter.
CEO tone. Sanjay Mehrotra: record Q3 and "even stronger outlook for Q4 reflect the strategic value of memory in the AI era"; the multi-year agreements "will significantly enhance the durability and predictability of Micron's strong financial performance."
Capital return. Quarterly dividend maintained at $0.15; the priority is investing "at record levels in technology, products and supply" to meet contracted demand.

The Move — Implied vs. Actual

Options underpriced it. The straddle implied roughly ±12% into the print (front-month IV ~155%). The actual after-hours reaction was +13–15% — the stock cleared the upper band, so anyone who sold premium expecting a fade was run over.
The history broke. MU had closed lower the session after earnings in 6 of its prior 8 reports despite beating every time — the classic "beat-and-fade." This time the combination of a 24%-above-guide quarter, a $50B FQ4 guide, and $100B of contracted revenue was too large to fade.

Where It Goes From Here — Bull vs. Bear

Bull — the next leg research target $1,400

The $100B of contracted revenue plus a shipping HBM4 roadmap give the cycle visibility it has never had. If FQ4 lands near the $50B / $31.00 guide and the FY27 setup holds, EPS is still climbing faster than the multiple — the ~9x forward keeps looking too cheap, and "durable, not cyclical" becomes the consensus. Continued new highs are on the table.

Bear — peak-cycle risk research target $650

After ~+750% and a +14% pop, the bar is perfection. The SCAs lock in minimum volumes, not prices — memory ASPs can still roll if AI capex digests or the three suppliers add too much capacity. A richly-priced name re-rates fast on the first sign the cycle is cresting; the risk has shifted from "miss the quarter" to "own the top."
